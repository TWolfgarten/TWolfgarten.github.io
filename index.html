Computational Epidemiologist & Behavioural Data Scientist
I model human behavior to predict and mitigate global health crises.

Currently a pre-viva PhD candidate at the University of Warwick’s Institute for Global Pandemic Planning (IGPP), my work bridges mathematical epidemiology and behavioural science. I specialise in transforming massive, individual-level mobile phone location datasets into actionable, infection risk models.

What I do:

  • Large-Scale Simulation: I engineer high-performance, stochastic simulations written in Julia and deployed across AWS infrastructure.

  • Advanced Statistical Modelling: Leveraging my background as a statistician (University of Warwick) and economist (University of Cologne), I build models that capture complex human dynamics, especially behavioral changes at critical points.

  • Cross-Disciplinary Collaboration: I work at the intersection of Warwick's SBIDER and WBS's Behavioural Science group, supervised by Matt Keeling and Neil Stewart.

Publications

  • Upcoming Paper: "Forecasting Pandemic Trajectories Using Individual-Level Location Data" - This core thesis work details the methodology of scaling from raw, individual-level location pings to full-scale stochastic pandemic forecasting.

  • Policy Impact: Author of the ASEF PHN Policy Brief: "Pandemic and the Economy – A Pandemic-Resilient Society" (A report for the Helsinki conference of the Asia-Europe Public Health Network, 2023).
