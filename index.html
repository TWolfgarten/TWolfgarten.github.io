Thomas Wolfgarten


BlueSky: @thomaswolfgarten.bsky.social, X: @TomWolfgarten

I am currently a PhD cand (pre-viva) at IGPP, the Institute for Global Pandemic Planning, at the University of Warwick. Besides IGPP, I am also associated with Warwick's SBIDER group and WBS's Behavioural Science group. Originally an economist (University of Cologne) and statistician (University of Warwick), my research focusses on measuring and modelling human behaviour during pandemics, using large scale data sets, like mobile phone location data. The project is multi-disciplinary, combining Mathematical Epidemiology (supervisor: Matt Keeling) and Behavioural Science (supervisor: Neil Stewart). Mostly creating large-scale simulations in Julia which run on AWS.

Publications

  • ASEF PHN Policy Brief: Pandemic and the Economy – A Pandemic-Resilient Society.
    A policy brief for the Helsinki conference of the Asia-Europe Public Health Network from 2023.

  • Currently preparing a paper "Forecasting Pandemic Trajectories Using Individual-Level Location Data" which will describe and implement the core methodology of my thesis, i.e. how to get from individual-level location data to a stochastic model of pandemic trajectories.
