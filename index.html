I am currently a PhD student at IGPP, the Institute for Global Pandemic Planning, at the University of Warwick. Originally an economist (University of Cologne) and statistician (University of Warwick), my research focusses on measuring and modelling human behaviour during pandemics, using large scale data sets, like mobile phone location data. The project is multi-disciplinary, combining Mathematical Epidemiology (supervisor: Matt Keeling) and Behavioural Science (supervisor: Neil Stewart). Besides IGPP, I am also associated with Warwick's SBIDER group and WBS's Behavioural Science group.
